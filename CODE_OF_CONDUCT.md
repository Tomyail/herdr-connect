# Code of Conduct

[简体中文](docs/zh-CN/CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in Herdr Connect a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, experience level, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We will act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Expected behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive community include:

- showing empathy and respect for different viewpoints and experiences;
- giving and accepting constructive feedback;
- taking responsibility, apologizing, and learning from mistakes;
- focusing on what is best for the project and community.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, sexualized language or attention, trolling or insulting comments, personal or political attacks, deliberate intimidation, and publishing another person's private information without explicit permission.

## Scope

This Code applies in project spaces and when an individual officially represents the project in public spaces. Project spaces include the repository, issue tracker, pull requests, reviews, and other official communication channels.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately through the repository's [private vulnerability reporting page](https://github.com/Tomyail/herdr-connect/security/advisories/new) and state that the report concerns community conduct. If that private form is unavailable, open a public issue containing only a request for private maintainer contact—do not include sensitive incident details.

Maintainers will review reports promptly, respect the reporter's privacy and safety, and take action that is fair and proportionate. Possible responses include a correction, warning, temporary restriction, or permanent removal from project spaces. Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this Code may be removed from the project team.
